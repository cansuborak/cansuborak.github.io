Mash ATMs Sales Analysis

Mash ATMs Sales Analysis



Project Goal

The objective of this project is to conduct a thorough analysis of Mash ATMs' sales performance by assessing critical indicators such as product and customer profitability, temporal sales trends, and market segmentation. This analysis is intended to inform strategic decisions and improve profitability across various dimensions, including product pricing, regional market contributions, and customer demographics.

Project Set Up

A unified dataset was created during the project setup phase by integrating six tables using their corresponding keys. The data underwent a comprehensive cleaning process, including the identification of missing values, which were imputed with the corresponding column averages to ensure data integrity. Furthermore, a new column was generated to calculate the average customer age, which was then categorized into specific intervals, such as 0-25, 26-30, 31-35, and so on, to enable detailed and structured analysis.

Conclusion


The analysis of Mash ATMs' sales performance provided valuable insights into key profitability and sales trends. The findings identified the top-performing products, with the top five contributing 24.8% of total profit, while customer profitability was broadly distributed. Regional analysis highlighted that the United States and Australia together accounted for 62.7% of total profit, underscoring their significance as primary markets. Temporal analysis revealed that weekdays generated 72.0% of total profit, emphasizing the importance of weekday operations. Pricing analysis indicated that 95.4% of profits were derived from products priced above $150. These insights offer critical guidance for optimizing product strategies, refining market focus, and implementing targeted pricing approaches to enhance overall profitability.
